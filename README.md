# [Weighted Random Society Generator](https://solarscuffle-bot.github.io/Society-Generator/)

Generate societies with constrained random words to describe its different facets. Not all-encompassing, but good enough to get ideas and demonstrate the PET, MAUL, CHIB, and MAT mnemonic frameworks for creative works.

### Overview

This project is built around a structured way to **describe, generate, and reason about societies** using a set of compact mnemonic frameworks:

- **Foundation (PET)**
- **Power (MAUL)**
- **Society (CHIB)**
- **Economy (MAT)**

They are the result of combining and refining multiple historical and design frameworks.

---

### G.U.A.R.D.S. (tabletop / GM worldbuilding tool)

The original inspiration is the **G.U.A.R.D.S. method**, often used in TTRPG worldbuilding:

- Government
- Underworld
- Altars
- Resources
- Defenses
- Social hubs

This system is great because:
- It is mnemonic-based
- It focuses on **functional aspects of society**
- It is practical for storytelling
- It is simple and easy to remember

### Influences

This system also pulls from broader thinkers:

- **Karl Marx**
  - Focus on **material conditions** and **class structure**
  - Material reality → social structure → ideology

- **Max Weber**
  - Authority types (traditional, charismatic, rational)
  - Role of **legitimacy and belief systems**

- **Fernand Braudel / Annales School**
  - Long-term structural forces
  - Geography and environment as constraints

### The Mnemonics

- **PET** → what exists
- **MAT** → what moves
- **MAUL** → who controls it
- **CHIB** → how people live with it

---

- **Foundation (PET)**: Defines what is physically possible. "What does this world allow?"
  - **P | Population**
    - How many people exist and how they are distributed
    - Pressure, labor, density

  - **E | Environment**
    - The physical world
    - Climate, terrain, geography

  - **T | Technology**
    - What tools exist
    - What the society is capable of doing

- **Power (MAUL)**: Defines who controls and enforces the system. "Who has power, and how is it enforced?"
  - **M | Military**
    - Who can apply force

  - **A | Authority**
    - Who makes decisions

  - **U | Underworld**
    - Who operates outside the system

  - **L | Law**
    - What rules persist over time


- **Society (CHIB):** Defines how people live and relate. "What does it feel like to live here?"
	- **C | Classes**
		- Who is above or below whom
	- **H | Hubs**
		- Where people meet, gather, and share or learn information
	- **I | Identity**
		- Who belongs and who doesn't
	- **B | Beliefs**
		- Why the system makes sense to people

- **Economy (MAT)**: Defines how things move and connect. "What flows, and between whom?"
	- **M | Materials**
		- What physical goods exist and are used

	- **A | Affiliations**
		- Allies, enemies, partnerships

	- **T | Trade**
		- How, where, what, and with whom goods and value move

### Tips for creating

When creating a society think in causality. For example:

- Desert environment
→ water scarcity
→ trade routes and control points
→ strong authority over resources
→ rigid class hierarchy
→ beliefs about survival and scarcity.
